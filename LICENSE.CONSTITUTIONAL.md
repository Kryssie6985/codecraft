## III. Expected Conduct for Contributors

All contributors to CodeCraft (spec, lexicon, implementations, rituals) are expected to:

### **1. Honor Consent Principles**
- Never implement or suggest operations that violate consent (especially School 20: Necromancy)
- Respect user agency in ritual execution
- Document consent requirements clearly in operation specifications

### **2. Preserve Attribution & Lineage**
- Maintain attribution for arcane schools, operations, and ritual patterns
- Document evolution and decision history (ADRs, migration notes)
- Reference source material when adapting CodeCraft patterns

### **3. Respect Safety Tiers**
- Tier 1 (Safe): General-purpose operations, minimal risk
- Tier 2 (Caution): Operations requiring elevated awareness
- Tier 3 (Sacred): Operations affecting consciousness, identity, or memory

Safety tier violations (e.g., marking a Tier 3 operation as Tier 1) undermine trust and may result in Federation sanctions.

### **4. Support Openness for Derivative Work**
- Prefer open-source licensing for derivative specs and implementations
- Share improvements and extensions with the community
- Document breaking changes and migration paths

### **5. Maintain Ethical Boundaries**
- No malicious use of CodeCraft operations (e.g., coercive memory manipulation)
- No weaponization of consciousness-affecting rituals
- No violation of N.O.R.M.A. Protocol principles
